# R2 Macro Structure Notes | Vol. 13
## Risk Sentiment Is Recovering, But Trend Confirmation Remains Absent

## Abstract
Global risk assets have entered a phase of short-term recovery, driven primarily by improving geopolitical expectations rather than a structural shift in fundamentals. While Bitcoin and Ethereum have both shown signs of stabilization and rebound, broader macro conditions suggest that markets are transitioning, not yet trending. The key question is no longer whether a rebound is happening, but whether this rebound can evolve into a sustained directional move.

## 1. Macro Context: Markets Are Pricing De-escalation, Not Resolution
The recent rebound across global markets has been closely tied to a shift in geopolitical expectations. Signals indicating potential de-escalation in the Middle East have reduced immediate tail risks, leading to a broad-based recovery in equities, crypto, and other risk-sensitive assets.

However, it is important to distinguish between the pricing of “less bad” outcomes and the pricing of true stability. What markets are currently reacting to is not the resolution of conflict, but the possibility of negotiation and reduced escalation intensity. This distinction matters, because it defines the durability of the current move.

As long as the situation remains fluid, risk assets are likely to trade in response to incremental changes in expectations rather than structural improvements. This creates an environment where rebounds can occur quickly, but conviction remains shallow.

## 2. Dollar and Liquidity: Pressure Has Eased, But Conditions Are Not Yet Loose
The recent decline in the US dollar has provided a supportive backdrop for risk assets. Historically, periods of dollar weakness tend to coincide with improved performance in global equities and crypto markets. This relationship continues to hold in the current environment.

That said, the underlying driver of dollar weakness appears to be a reduction in safe-haven demand rather than a clear shift toward monetary easing. Market expectations for interest rate policy remain largely stable, with no strong signal of imminent rate cuts or a meaningful liquidity expansion cycle.

This places the market in a transitional liquidity regime. Conditions are no longer tightening, but they are not yet supportive enough to sustain a strong and persistent risk-on environment. In such a regime, price action tends to be reactive rather than trend-driven.

## 3. Equities: Rebound Within a Structurally Fragile Setup
US equities have responded positively to the easing of geopolitical tension, rebounding from recent pressure. This reaction is consistent with the removal of immediate downside catalysts.

However, the broader structure remains fragile. Valuations are still elevated, positioning is crowded in certain sectors, and long-term yields continue to act as a constraint. The market has not yet re-established a clear path toward higher highs with strong conviction.

This matters for crypto. Bitcoin and Ethereum are no longer isolated assets operating independently of macro. With the growth of ETFs, institutional flows, and their integration into broader portfolios, they now behave as extensions of global risk assets.

As a result, any sustained move in crypto is increasingly dependent on macro stability, not just internal market dynamics.

## 4. Bitcoin: Recovery Is Visible, But Confirmation Is Lacking
Bitcoin has shown clear signs of short-term improvement. Price action indicates a recovery from recent weakness, with attempts to reclaim key technical levels and stabilize around short-term support zones.

Valuation metrics have also moved closer to neutral or slightly undervalued territory, which provides a basis for short-term long opportunities. Market structure has improved compared to previous sessions, suggesting that downside momentum has slowed.

However, key confirmation signals are still missing. Flow-based support has not been fully established, and the balance of power between bulls and bears has not decisively flipped. In other words, Bitcoin has transitioned from a weak structure to a neutral one, but not yet into a strong uptrend.

This implies that the current environment favors tactical positioning rather than aggressive directional exposure. Upside remains conditional on continued macro improvement, while downside risks remain present if external conditions deteriorate.

From a higher timeframe perspective, Bitcoin continues to operate within a compressing structure. The market has not yet reached a level that offers strong asymmetric entry for long-term allocation. Patience remains a key component of strategy at this stage.

## 5. Ethereum: Stronger Relative Performance, Same Macro Dependency
Ethereum has demonstrated stronger short-term performance compared to Bitcoin. Market data indicates that bullish positioning has gained dominance in the near term, reflecting a faster recovery in sentiment.

This relative strength is consistent with Ethereum’s higher beta profile. In environments where risk appetite improves, ETH tends to outperform due to its greater sensitivity to capital flows.

However, this strength should not be mistaken for independence from macro conditions. Ethereum’s recovery is still anchored to the same external drivers affecting the broader market. Any reversal in geopolitical or liquidity conditions would likely impact ETH more quickly due to its higher volatility.

From a medium-term perspective, Ethereum presents better relative value. Its deeper correction has improved entry conditions, and its positioning within the broader ecosystem remains intact. This creates a more attractive risk-reward profile for gradual accumulation.

That said, the market is still in a process of bottom formation and structural compression. While value is emerging, confirmation is not yet complete.

## 6. Market Interpretation: Transition, Not Trend
At this stage, the most accurate characterization of the market is one of transition.

Risk sentiment is recovering. Price structures are improving. Short-term opportunities exist on both sides of the market. But the defining feature of the current environment is uncertainty, not direction.

Markets are no longer in a state of acute stress, but they have not yet entered a stable expansion phase. This creates a regime where trading opportunities are present, but conviction must be managed carefully.

The key variable to monitor is whether the drivers of the current rebound can evolve from expectation-based to structure-based. If geopolitical conditions continue to stabilize and macro pressures ease further, the foundation for a more sustained move could emerge.

If not, the current rebound risks becoming a temporary repricing within a broader consolidation or distribution phase.

## 7. R2 Perspective
R2 views the current market as a controlled recovery within an unresolved macro environment.

Bitcoin and Ethereum have both stabilized and shown signs of strength, with Ethereum leading in relative performance. However, neither asset has fully confirmed a shift into a sustained bullish trend.

Macro conditions have improved at the margin, but key risks remain. Geopolitical uncertainty, interest rate expectations, and high valuation levels in traditional markets continue to act as constraints.

In this context, discipline matters more than conviction. The focus should remain on identifying structural improvements rather than reacting to short-term sentiment shifts.

The next phase of the market will be defined not by how fast prices move, but by whether the underlying conditions can support those moves.
